What are setup fixtures?
Setup fixtures are specialized tools designed for the precise placement and securing of products on manufacturing machines. Critical for operations such as precise machining, drilling, and milling, these devices not only increase the accuracy of processes but also significantly reduce the time needed for equipment setup.
Our fixture design and manufacturing process
The process of making setup fixtures has the following stages:

Based on gathered information, our engineers develop customized solutions, optimizing each aspect of the fixture for your specific case.
Prototyping and testing
Each prototype is tested to ensure its functionality and reliability.

Fixtures are divided into
  • Universal Fixtures (UF) are multifunctional tools that allow processing of various parts without setup and clamping elements replacement.
  • Special Fixtures are specifically designed for processing certain types of parts. Additionally there are adjustable fixtures, including Universal Setup Fixtures (USF) and Special Setup Fixtures (SSF), which can be adapted for use in batch production. These devices are used at various stages of production, from metal cutting to quality control and assembly, providing reliable fixation of parts and assemblies.

Design and calculation

The design and calculation of machine tooling fixtures is a complex process involving the preparation and analysis of initial data, development of layout and structure of the fixture, and calculations for accuracy and strength. The structure includes setup elements, clamping devices, guides for tools, and power mechanisms for activating clamping devices.

Every aspect of the design serves to create an efficient and reliable fixture that fully meets both current and future production needs.
