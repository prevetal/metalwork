What is an assembly fixture?
An assembly fixture is a specialized device designed for precise positioning and securing of components during assembly. It uniquely ensures the correct connection of parts, which leads to improved product quality and reduced chances for failures. Such devices are necessary in multiple industries, including automotive, aviation, electronics, and many others.

Purpose and types of assembly fixtures
Assembly fixtures are essential components in the assembly process of various products. They are effective tools for automating and mechanizing manual assembly, and an integral part of both standard and automated assembly equipment. Such devices play a crucial role in increasing productivity and ease of assembly,providing quick and reliable fixation of components during mating.
Classification of assembly fixtures

Assembly fixtures used in manufacturing can be divided into two categories: universal and special. Universal fixtures are used in single and small batch production and include various elements such as plates, prisms, angles, clamps, jacks, and other auxiliary parts. They are mainly made of cast iron and equipped with T-shaped slots, which ensure assembly accuracy, allowing them to be set up and aligned on the foundation.

Special fixtures are used in large-scale and mass production for specific assembly operations. They are classified into single-station and multi-station, with the latter increasing productivity by reducing the time needed for setting up products.

Fixtures for stationary installation and securing play a crucial role in the assembly process. They are designed to secure base parts and assemblies, providing the necessary stability and increasing labor productivity by freeing workers from the need to manually hold parts. Such fixtures can be either stationary or mobile, depending on production requirements.
