What is a tilter?
A tilter is an integral engineering solution, specifically designed for mechanically flipping large, bulky objects in production. These indispensable accessories in industrial operations provide reliability and efficiency in assembly, processing, and packaging processes, ensuring not only comfort but also the safety of workers.
Types of tilters
Tilters are offered in various models, depending on several criteria to select the most suitable equipment for specific production tasks.
  • Book-type models are the optimal choice for working with rolls of materials or coils. They provide reliable inversion of loads by 90 degrees, significantly simplifying their engagement and subsequent processing.
  • Rotators are necessary for rotating parts around their axis. They are widely used in welding works or painting when access to all sides of the object is required.
  • Chain and roller tilters specialize in handling loads with an offset center of gravity. They are perfect for moving large and complex objects such as metal structures or heavy machine units.
  • Hydraulic tilters are designed for working with particularly heavy loads, using hydraulic drive to provide powerful and controlled rotation. They feature high load capacity and precision control.
  • Electric tilters boast a high degree of automation and can be integrated into automated production lines. They prove to be very effective in continuous and rapid processing of products.
  • Such varieties of tilters are indispensable tools in various industries where precision and efficiency in material processing are priorities.

Applications of Tilters
  • Tilters play a crucial role in various industries, enhancing the efficiency and safety of production processes.
  • In the automotive industry, tilters are used for handling car bodies, engines, and other large components, optimizing assembly lines and increasing assembly accuracy.
  • In the aviation industry, tilters are used for handling large and heavy parts of aviation equipment, such as fuselages, wings, and engines. They ensure precise positioning of components during assembly and maintenance of the aircraft.
  • In heavy machinery, tilters are used for turning and positioning large and heavy metal constructions, such as beams, frames, and press molds. They simplify assembly, welding, and other processing operations.
  • The metalworking industry requires tilters for handling heavy metal workpieces, simplifying operations such as milling, welding, and painting.
  • In shipbuilding and repair, tilters facilitate the lifting and turning of large ship elements, which is critical for assembly and repair processes.
  • In the energy sector, tilters are used during the assembly and maintenance of large energy installations, including wind turbines and components for nuclear power stations.
  • The construction equipment manufacturing industry requires tilters for handling heavy and bulky parts of construction machinery, such as excavator buckets and crane beams.
  • Tilters from EA Metalwork Engineering India designed to meet the needs of each specific industry, offering unique solutions that consider all the operations features and requirements.
