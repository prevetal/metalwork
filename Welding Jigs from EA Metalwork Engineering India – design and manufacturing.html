What is a welding jig?
A welding jig is an essential engineering solution designed specifically for the mechanical turning of bulky, large items in production. These indispensable accessories in industrial operations provide reliability and efficiency in assembly, processing, and packaging processes, ensuring not only comfort but also workers' safety.
Types of welding jigs
Welding jigs come in various versions, depending on different criteria, to select the most suitable equipment for specific production tasks.
  • Book-type models are optimal for working with material rolls or coils. They provide reliable 90-degree turnover of loads, significantly simplifying their engagement and subsequent processing.
  • Rotators are necessary for rotating parts around their axis. They are widely used in welding or painting when access to all sides of an object is required.
  • Chain and roller jigs specialize in handling loads with an offset center of gravity. They are perfect for moving large and complex objects, such as metal structures or heavy machine units.
  • Hydraulic jigs are designed for handling particularly heavy loads, using a hydraulic drive to provide powerful and controlled rotation. They feature high load capacity and precision control.
  • Electric jigs offer a high degree of automation and can be integrated into automated production lines. They prove highly effective in continuous and rapid processing of products.

These varieties of jigs are indispensable tools in various industrial sectors where precision and efficiency in material handling are priorities.
