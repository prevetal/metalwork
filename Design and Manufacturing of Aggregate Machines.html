What is an aggregate machine?
An aggregate machine is a specialized tool designed for precise positioning and fixation of parts during welding operations. The main goal of these devices is to ensure stable and accurate positioning of parts, which in turn helps minimize welding errors and defects. Thanks to a rigid frame with stops, fixators, and clamps, aggregate machines provide the necessary precision and reliability in the welding process.

Our company offers aggregate machines with various clamping systems—manual and automatic, including pneumatic, hydraulic, electromagnetic, and vacuum. Applications of aggregate machines
Aggregate machines are widely used in various industries, including automotive, shipbuilding, and construction. They excel in assembling and welding large-scale structures, such as bridges, scaffolding, and complex metal constructions. Additionally, due to their versatility, these machines are extensively used in the aerospace industry and the production of energy installations, where high precision and adherence to strict technical standards are required. For instance, they are used in manufacturing turbines, large pipelines, and pressure vessels. Given this, aggregate machines are an indispensable tool in projects requiring maximum precision and compliance with strict technical norms.

Modular machine tools: diversity and functionality
Unilateral modular machine tools
Unilateral modular machine tools are available in various configurations to meet unique production needs:
  • Horizontal single-spindle. Ideal for performing single-position tasks.
  • Vertical single-spindle. Ensures precision in vertical machining.
  • Inclined single-spindle. Optimized for specialized angled operations.
  • Multi-spindle single-position. With multiple power heads for increased productivity.
  • Multi-spindle multi-position. Designed for complex machining, allowing operations from multiple sides simultaneously.
Multilateral modular machine tools
These machines expand machining capabilities, enabling work on parts from multiple sides:
  • Horizontal and vertical single-position. For efficient one-sided machining.
  • Combined single-position. Combines several processes in one device.
  • Multi-position machines. Ideal for sequential and complex machining of workpieces in automated flows.
Application and layout

Modular machine tools can operate as standalone units or as part of automated lines, facilitating the machining of stationary workpieces with multiple tools. This speeds up work processes, allowing tools to simultaneously move and process products from various sides.

Standardized components are used to develop such machines, which significantly reduces development time and simplifies manufacturing. This approach enhances adaptability to changing production tasks and allows for the creation of modular machines using a minimal number of unique parts, thereby streamlining and standardizing technological processes.

Modular machine tools can be classified based on the sizes of the workpieces they handle, allowing for three main categories. Each category is characterized by unique dimensions, weight, and design of standardized components.

Small modular machine tools

These machines are equipped with smaller spindle heads ranging from 0.18 to 0.75 kW, ideal for precise and fine work.

Medium modular machine tools

Machines in this category are fitted with cam-driven power heads providing feed power from 1.1 to 3 kW, suitable for moderately complex tasks.

Large modular machine tools

These machines include hydraulic or electromechanical tables designed to accommodate spindle units, making them ideal for processing large and heavy workpieces.

Additionally, modular machine tools can further be classified based on structural features, considering different performance requirements and processing specifications. This allows for the selection of the most suitable model for specific production needs.

Linear type modular machine tools

Linear type modular machine tools should be noted for their leading role in the effective classification of machine equipment. These mechanisms are ideally suited for performing drilling, milling, and threading operations, thanks to their special design, which does not involve a fixed base. Modular machine tools consist of a frame equipped with guides, an electric motor, and a tool holder, enabling the workpiece to be secured in a device designed for this purpose.

In modern industry, the flexibility of equipment combined with flawless performance is especially valued. CNC modular machine tools excellently meet these demands, standing out as a separate group due to their adaptability and smooth operation.
